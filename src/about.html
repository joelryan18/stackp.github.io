Software should have reflexes.

AXON started with a pager going off at 3:14 a.m. — a failed payment queue, a runbook everyone knew by heart, and a human doing, half-asleep, what a machine should have done in forty milliseconds. We were that human. Too many times.

The tools existed: APIs for everything, models that could reason, infrastructure that could act. What was missing was the connective tissue — something that could sense an event, reason about it across the whole stack, act with guardrails, and explain exactly what it did. A nervous system.

What we believe

  • Autonomy needs an audit trail. An agent you can't inspect is a liability, not a teammate. Every AXON run produces a full reasoning trace.
  • Guardrails are a feature, not friction. Scoped permissions and human approval gates are why our users trust agents with production.
  • Latency is a moral issue. Every second an incident runs unhandled is user harm. We engineer for microseconds.
  • Marketing at product tolerance. The numbers on our homepage are design targets we hold ourselves to — never inflated.

The instrument, not the magic

We deliberately talk about AXON like a lab instrument rather than a black box. Instruments are calibrated, specified, and trusted because they can be verified. That's the bar for autonomous software too — read the datasheet, watch a live trace, and audit any run down to the token.

The team

We're a small group of infrastructure engineers, ML researchers, and ops survivors — people who carried pagers at Meridian, Quanta Labs, and Northwind before deciding the pager itself was the bug. We work instrument-close to our users: every access request is read by a human on the team.

Work with us

If reading a reasoning trace makes your pulse tick up, we'd like to meet you. Say hello via the contact page — tell us the worst 3 a.m. incident you ever handled and what you wish had handled it for you.
