stackwith.me is a one-person studio shipping real products in public — agents that act, trackers that remember, and the engineering notes that explain how it all works.

01 / WHO

One person. Real products. No theater.

Most studio pages list a team of forty and a wall of awards. This one lists a single builder who writes the code, answers the mail, carries the pager, and publishes the postmortems. Everything on this domain — the AXON agent platform, the Stackime anime tracker, the blog — is designed, built, deployed, and operated by one pair of hands, in the open.

That constraint is the point. When one person owns the whole stack, there is nowhere for quality to hide: every latency number is measured, every guardrail is tested, every claim on a landing page has a commit behind it. The work ships small, ships often, and ships honest.

03 / ORIGIN

It started at 3:14 a.m.

AXON — the studio's flagship — started with a pager going off at 3:14 a.m.: a failed payment queue, a runbook everyone knew by heart, and a human doing, half-asleep, what a machine should have done in forty milliseconds. We were that human. Too many times.

The tools existed: APIs for everything, models that could reason, infrastructure that could act. What was missing was the connective tissue — something that could sense an event, reason about it across the whole stack, act with guardrails, and explain exactly what it did. A nervous system. So we built one, and we talk about it like a lab instrument rather than a black box: calibrated, specified, and trusted because it can be verified — read the datasheet, watch a live trace, audit any run down to the token.

04 / PRINCIPLES

  1. P·01

    Autonomy needs an audit trail.

    An agent you can't inspect is a liability, not a teammate. Every AXON run produces a full reasoning trace.

  2. P·02

    Guardrails are a feature, not friction.

    Scoped permissions and human approval gates are why our users trust agents with production.

  3. P·03

    Latency is a moral issue.

    Every second an incident runs unhandled is user harm. We engineer for microseconds.

  4. P·04

    Marketing at product tolerance.

    The numbers on our pages are design targets we hold ourselves to — never inflated.
