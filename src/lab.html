[ LAB · 01 ]

Deep Signal

A descent through a growing crystal shaft — five chapters straight down, from the frozen surface to a beating core and back up into the light. This is the stackwith.me lab: one WebGL world, no page around it.

Unlike everything else on this domain, Deep Signal is built from authored, baked assets rather than procedural geometry. Six crystal shard variants and a bevelled hero gem are modelled headlessly in Blender, exported to glTF, and Draco-compressed to seven kilobytes. The entire lighting model is a single studio matcap baked in Cycles and shipped as a KTX2 texture. At runtime, ~1,600 instanced shards grow out of the shaft wall as you scroll past their birth depth — the same trick that makes igloo.inc's crystals feel alive.

The rest of the instrument panel is shared with the About journey: a keyframed camera on a Catmull-Rom rail, in-world SDF typography, a filmic master grade with per-chapter color, a synthesized soundscape behind the SND toggle, and an adaptive quality governor that trades resolution for frame time on whatever machine is in front of it.

Your browser is showing the reading version — the full descent needs WebGL, motion, and a viewport wider than 680px. The engineering notes behind this build land on the blog.
