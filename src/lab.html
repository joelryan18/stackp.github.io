[ LAB · 01 ]

Deep Signal

A descent through a growing crystal shaft — five chapters straight down, from the frozen surface to a beating core and back up into the light. This is the stackwith.me lab: one WebGL world, no page around it.

Unlike everything else on this domain, Deep Signal is built from authored, baked assets rather than procedural geometry. Six high-poly quartz-habit crystal variants — beveled hexagonal prisms carrying growth striations, stepped pyramidal terminations and parasite micro-crystals, several hundred to a few thousand triangles each — plus a matching low-detail LOD set and a dense bevelled hero gem are modelled headlessly in Blender, exported to glTF, and Draco-compressed to under forty kilobytes. The entire lighting model is a pair of 1024² Cycles-baked matcaps shipped as KTX2 textures: an exterior studio-ice map sampled by surface normal, and an interior refraction map sampled three times along chromatically dispersed rays, which is what gives every facet real internal light with spectral fringing at the edges. At runtime, ~1,600 instanced shards grow out of the shaft wall as you scroll past their birth depth — the same trick that makes igloo.inc's crystals feel alive — and only the depth bands near the camera are drawn at full detail; distant bands swap to the LOD set deep inside the fog.

The world also answers back: sweeping — or, on touch screens, dragging a finger — strikes the shaft wall and nearby crystals ring in sequence, a deliberate tap cascading to its neighbours a beat later like struck glass, each with a swell, a tinted glow and a pitched chime panned to where you touched. Inside THE CORE the pointer becomes an instrument: hold to charge the heart — the surrounding field visibly inhales toward the lens while you feed it — then release to send one shockwave through every crystal, and just past the source the transmission itself strains, tearing the frame into brief scanline rips. The descent is framed as a recovery mission: a boot transcript logs the real initialisation milestones, a HUD meter counts your depth in metres, and each of the five chapters arrives with its own climate — its own fog, air color and accent that retint the entire interface from ice through cyan, magenta and lime to pearl — announced by a decoded transmission line. The camera itself rides a hand-rolled damped spring, so every scroll lands with weight and a breath of overshoot. The rest of the instrument panel is shared with the About journey: in-world SDF typography, a filmic master grade, a synthesized soundscape behind the SND toggle, and an adaptive quality governor that trades resolution for frame time on whatever machine is in front of it.

Your browser is showing the reading version — the full descent needs WebGL and motion enabled (on phones it runs a lighter cut of the same world). The engineering notes behind this build land on the blog.
