[ LAB · 01 ]

Deep Signal

A descent through a growing crystal shaft — five chapters straight down, from the frozen surface to a beating core and back up into the light. This is the stackwith.me lab: one WebGL world, no page around it.

Unlike everything else on this domain, Deep Signal is built from authored, baked assets rather than procedural geometry. Six quartz-habit crystal variants — irregular hexagonal prisms with asymmetric pyramidal tips, including twinned clusters — and a bevelled hero gem are modelled headlessly in Blender, exported to glTF, and Draco-compressed to a few kilobytes. The entire lighting model is a pair of Cycles-baked matcaps shipped as KTX2 textures: an exterior studio-ice map sampled by surface normal, and an interior refraction map sampled along the bent ray, which is what gives every facet real internal light. At runtime, ~1,600 instanced shards grow out of the shaft wall as you scroll past their birth depth — the same trick that makes igloo.inc's crystals feel alive — and only the depth bands near the camera are drawn at all.

The rest of the instrument panel is shared with the About journey: a keyframed camera on a Catmull-Rom rail, in-world SDF typography, a filmic master grade with per-chapter color, a synthesized soundscape behind the SND toggle, and an adaptive quality governor that trades resolution for frame time on whatever machine is in front of it.

Your browser is showing the reading version — the full descent needs WebGL, motion, and a viewport wider than 680px. The engineering notes behind this build land on the blog.
